Tonight's drills

How it works
  1. Plan the night. Set the times and player numbers above — the app works out the groups, the rounds, and how long each group gets on a drill court.
  2. Pick drills. Add them from the Drills tab. Tap the chip on each one to choose where it runs: warm-up (Start), the drill courts (Rounds), or warm-down (End).
  3. Run it. Tap Start and follow the big timer. When a round's time is up it chimes — swap the groups and tap Next. The water break tells you exactly how many players to grab.
  4. Things change? Fine. Running late shortens the remaining rounds automatically so the night still ends on time. Extra players walked in? Bump the head count on this tab, even mid-session.
The fine print

The maths

Players ÷ group size = groups. Groups ÷ drill courts = rounds. Round length = time left after warm-up, warm-down and breaks, shared equally. Odd leftover players form their own small group (default) or merge into the others up to a max size — see More settings.

The timing

The night is anchored to the finish time. Each round's length is worked out the moment it starts, from the time left. Held up 5 minutes? The remaining rounds each get a bit shorter. Finished early? They get longer. The warm-down is never shortened.

Rounds never advance themselves — the clock counts up in red until you tap Next, so a chat with a parent can't derail the schedule. Badly behind? Cut a round: one group misses drills, the rest keep round lengths worth having.

Drills

The rounds hold one drill for now — picking another from the library swaps it. Warm-up and warm-down split their minutes evenly across their drills. Every drill has an easier and harder variation for weaker and stronger groups.
